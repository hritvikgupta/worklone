# Company Customers and Their Requirements

## Overview
This document outlines the primary customer groups our company serves, their core needs, pain points, and the requirements our product or service must fulfill to create value for them.

---

## 1. Customer Segments

### 1.1 Startups
**Profile:** Early-stage companies looking for fast, cost-effective solutions.

**Requirements:**
- Affordable pricing
- Quick onboarding
- Easy-to-use product experience
- Scalable features as they grow
- Responsive customer support

**Pain Points:**
- Limited budget
- Small teams with limited technical resources
- Need to move quickly without long setup cycles

---

### 1.2 Small and Medium Businesses (SMBs)
**Profile:** Growing businesses that need reliable solutions to improve efficiency and operations.

**Requirements:**
- Reliable and stable platform
- Moderate customization options
- Integration with existing tools
- Clear reporting and analytics
- Good support and training resources

**Pain Points:**
- Disconnected tools and workflows
- Limited internal technical expertise
- Need for measurable ROI

---

### 1.3 Enterprise Customers
**Profile:** Large organizations requiring security, compliance, and scalable infrastructure.

**Requirements:**
- Enterprise-grade security
- Compliance and governance controls
- High availability and performance
- Advanced customization
- Dedicated account management
- Role-based access control
- Audit logs and reporting

**Pain Points:**
- Complex procurement and implementation processes
- Strict security and compliance standards
- Need for cross-team adoption

---

### 1.4 Agencies and Service Providers
**Profile:** Teams managing multiple clients and projects at the same time.

**Requirements:**
- Multi-client or multi-workspace support
- Permission controls
- White-labeling or branding options
- Efficient collaboration tools
- Centralized reporting across accounts

**Pain Points:**
- Managing multiple stakeholders
- Maintaining client-specific workflows
- Need for efficiency at scale

---

## 2. Core Customer Requirements

Across all customer groups, the most common requirements include:

- **Ease of use:** The platform should be intuitive and require minimal training.
- **Reliability:** Customers expect a stable, dependable experience.
- **Scalability:** The solution should support growth in users, data, and workflows.
- **Security:** Protection of customer data is essential.
- **Support:** Fast and effective customer assistance must be available.
- **Integration:** The product should connect smoothly with commonly used tools.
- **Measurable value:** Customers want to clearly understand the business impact.

---

## 3. Functional Requirements

To satisfy customer needs, the company should provide:

- User-friendly onboarding flow
- Dashboard for visibility and reporting
- Customizable workflows
- Role-based user permissions
- Integration capabilities with third-party tools
- Secure data storage and access control
- Documentation and self-serve help center
- Customer support channels for issue resolution

---

## 4. Non-Functional Requirements

The solution should also meet these quality expectations:

- High uptime and availability
- Fast response times
- Data privacy and compliance readiness
- Scalable architecture
- Maintainable and extensible system design
- Consistent user experience across devices

---

## 5. Success Criteria

We can consider customer requirements successfully met when:

- Customers can onboard with minimal friction
- Users can complete key workflows without confusion
- Support tickets decrease over time
- Customer retention improves
- Customers can clearly identify return on investment
- Enterprise customers pass security and compliance reviews
